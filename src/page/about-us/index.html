A ring, first.

Vilo began with a small rebellion: we refused to design a ring from a circuit board out.

No “sensor bump,” no gadget silhouette, no apologizing for looking like tech.

We started from the ring itself — its history, its intimacy, the way it lives on a hand. Rings have always carried more than function: beauty, symbolism, memory, identity. They were never invented to monitor you. They were worn because they mattered.

So we built a ring you’d want to wear even if it tracked nothing — a piece that earns a second look, fits beside real jewelry, and belongs in daily life without asking permission. Then we hid the intelligence inside.

Vilo is built by the founder of Vincross and Vesta — from companion robotics to sleep and close-to-body comfort. Different worlds, same obsession: quiet support that lasts.
